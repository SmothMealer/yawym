We all know tortoise wins the race. Is there any hope for us hares?

Comeuppance for the arrogant long ear is satisfying for some people. Not for me. The hare started strong, got distracted, and lost. I know that story from my own life.

I see it in the shop. Good work takes planning, focus, and patience while the varnish cures. Sometimes I pull it off. Often I don't.

Perseverance and patience are not the same. I have plenty of the first. Give me a mountain to climb and I'll climb it. It's the second one that gets me. When a project is tantalizingly close to completion, it's all but impossible for me to wait that one extra day or add one last coat.

I have a side project now. I worry I'll chase the next idea before it's done. Or finish it and let it die before it pays for itself.

The hare didn't hit the wall. He could have won. He knew he had time for a detour and a nap. He just wouldn't do the math to work out exactly how long he could afford. It felt beneath him to plan ahead. He lost.

I am not a planner. Planning means telling my future what to do. My future self hates that as much as I do.

A third of the time I'm a space cadet, floating amongst big ideas. Another third I'm in tractor pull mode, dragging an idea uphill as far as I can before the motor stalls. I can be very productive in that mode. The last third I'm useless.

Like I said. I'm a hare.

But just because the tortoise won that race doesn't mean that tortoises win all races. Give us a sprint. A zigzag drill. A tractor pull.

Don was the best CAD guy I ever worked with. Slow and careful in the short run. Faster than anyone in the long run. Perfect craftsmanship every time. We could always trust Don.

Admiring tortoises can't make me into one. But a few things can help.

A due date helps. Far enough to build something, close enough to hold my interest. Other people watching helps. Even just telling friends what I'm up to can be enough accountability to keep me motivated. But I have to keep it simple.

I run marathons that way. One race date. Visible to others. Show up and train every day.

Parallel projects help too. When one project tests my patience, a pre-defined parallel project gives me a productive channel for that restlessness.

Is there hope for us hares? Sure.

But not by becoming a tortoise. I'm still the hare. Writing a blog post while the varnish cures, instead of chasing the next big idea.
