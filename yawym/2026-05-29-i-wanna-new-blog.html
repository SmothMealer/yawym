2026-05-29

I'm starting a project. I'll write about it here. Here's why.

Socials? Nah. I'm good.

Substack? I'm open to it if it comes to that. Not yet.

As my friend Mike would say, "start with the simplest thing that can work."

I'm going off grid. Hand-written. Static html.
No tricks, no weapons. Skill against skill alone.
The 90s internet was a better internet anyway.

No likes. No subscribes. No comment section. You want me? I'm right here.

It definitely seems silly in 2026. If my goal is to be heard, social media is the obvious choice. If my goal is to make money, substack would be a much faster path.

Those are not my goals right now. And I hope doing things in an unconventional way helps to make that clear.

For now, writing is a way of forcing myself to think more clearly. Over time I want to develop an authentic voice in a small community of real people.

And if my goal is to build a small community of people who make things, algorithmic feeds and anonymous comment sections are the wrong tool for the job. To the extent that I want to have an engage with anyone who might read this, I want to engage with them directly.

By hard coding the content I'm building a written record of my thinking at a point in time. A fun side effect of that choice is that the visual style of each page will evolve over time. This page will always look the way that it does. Future entries may look different.

For now I'm going with pure browser defaults (with a max-width--I'm not an animal). If I add style sheets over time, we'll see that progression in successive entries. It's a nod to what I'm trying to do here more broadly: to make things in public. Maybe if I do that I'll find other people who want to do that too.

I do worry that students may find it off-putting. For an aging elder-millenial, static html and email can be comfortingly nostalgic. I expect it won't feel that way to everyone. Some may even find the choice exclusionary. Another old man who thinks things were better in the past.

I don't actually think things were better in the past. They weren't. But that doesn't mean there weren't any good things in the past. This project is ultimately about making things by hand. If anything is anachronistic, making wooden furniture by hand certainly is. But it's fun and interesting and for some people, I think, important.

If mentoring and building into the lives of younger people is one of my goals, I may need to find a way to meet them where they are. But I have to start somewhere. For me, it starts here.
